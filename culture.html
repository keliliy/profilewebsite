CHINESE NEW YEAR

Chinese New Year is a 15 day festival that happens every year. It marks the beginning of a lunar new year and is usually celebrated between January 21 or February 20. It is celebrated by Chinese people, Sinophone communities, and even non-chinese speaking groups around the world. The celebration is rooted from the legend of Nian, and the items used to scare it off were intergrated in Chinese New Year. The festival is usually celebrated by people of chinese origin,but Traditions include red envelopes, firecrackers, dragon and lion dances. Red decorations symbolize good luck, and visiting relatives and exchanging blessings are customary.

DRAGON BOAT FESTIVAL

The Dragon Boat Festival is a Chinese holiday that is primarily celebrated by the Chinese on the fifth day of the fifth lunar month. In honor of a famous poet called Qu Yuan who threw himself into the river as a protest against corruption, individuals take part in Dragon Boat races, symbolizing the effort to search for his body, and attempt to save him. The festival also includes traditions such as hanging herbs to ward of evil spirits, and wearing perfumed medicine bags for protection. As well as herbs and medicine bags, food such as zongzi, is prepared and consumed, which are said to be thrown in the river to prevent Qu Yuan 's body from being eaten by the fish.
